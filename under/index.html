MIDI Sequencer SuperGnome

A generative euclidean sequencer for REAPER — up to 8 drum lanes (built-in sample playback or MIDI out) plus bass, melody and chord synths, all hanging off a master key. Scales from Dorian to Pelog to quarter-tone Rast, harmonic progressions from doo-wop to Coltrane, swing, nudge, per-lane filters and LFOs. Everything phase-locked to project beat 0.

Ships with a little DR-55 starter kit (kick, snare, rim) that loads on lanes 1–3 — it makes sound the moment you press play. Works on Windows, macOS and Linux; it's a JSFX, so there's nothing to compile.

Install (the easy way, via ReaPack)

  1. Install ReaPack if you don't have it — download the file for your OS and drop it into the folder that Options → Show REAPER resource path opens, inside UserPlugins. Restart REAPER.
  2. In REAPER: Extensions → ReaPack → Import repositories… and paste:
https://mima.chat/under/index.xml
  1. Extensions → ReaPack → Browse packages…, find MIDI Sequencer SuperGnome, right-click → Install, then OK. The samples come along automatically.
  2. Insert it on a track: FX button → search supergnome → JS: MIDI Sequencer SuperGnome. Press play.

Updates arrive through ReaPack too — Synchronize packages pulls whatever is newest down here.

Install (the stubborn way, by hand)

Grab the files:

  • 🎛️ sequencer_supergnome.jsfx
  • 🥁 dr55_bd.wav · dr55_sn.wav · dr55_rim.wav

Open Options → Show REAPER resource path, then:

• the .jsfx goes anywhere inside Effects/
• the wavs go in Data/supergnome/ (create the folder)

The sample picker reads Data/supergnome/ — drop any of your own WAVs in there and they show up on the lane sliders too.

Quick start

Press play. The three lanes are already sequencing the DR-55 kit; PULS and ROT shape the euclidean pattern, + LANE grows the kit. Un-mute B / M / C for bass, melody and chords, hit RND to generate phrases, and tap PROG on the KEY row to send the whole thing around a progression. The full manual lives at the top of the JSFX file — Edit button in the FX window.
